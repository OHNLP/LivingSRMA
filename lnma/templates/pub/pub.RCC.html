First line treatment of metastatic Renal Cell Carcinoma( mRCC): A Living, Interactive Systematic Review (LISR) and Network Meta-Analysis (LNMA)

The landscape of treatment options in first line treatment of metastatic renal cell cancer is rapidly changing. Three large randomized controlled trials studying different combinations of checkpoint inhibitors and anti-angiogenesis therapy were conducted in the same time period and several other are under way. JAVELIN Renal 101 and IMmotion151 showed that avelumab plus axitinib (AA) and atezolizumab plus bevacizumab (AB), respectively, improved PFS whereas KEYNOTE-426 showed that Pembrolizumab plus axitinib (PA) improved both PFS and OS when compared to sunitinib. Similarly, as indicated by a press release April 20th, 2020, CheckMate 9ER, a pivotal Phase 3 trial evaluating nivolumab in combination cabozantinib compared to sunitinib met its primary endpoint of progression-free survival (PFS) at final analysis, as well as the secondary endpoints of overall survival (OS) at a pre-specified interim analysis, and objective response rate (ORR). Confronted with this evidence, it is a challenging task to decide amongst several frontline options in patients with previously untreated mRCC. Direct comparisons between different combinations of check point inhibitors and anti-angiogenesis therapy were not assessed in head-to-head trials. Therefore, we conducted a network meta-analysis (NMA), which allows for comparing all treatments with each other, even if randomized controlled trials are not available for some treatment comparisons.

SUMMARY OF FINDINGS: PAIRWISE META-ANALYSIS

A Summary of Findings (SoF) table is designed to summarize the key results of a pairwise meta-analysis and to evaluate your confidence in the estimates of effect. The table summarizes the results for patient important outcomes for patients receiving direct acting anti-coagulant (DOACs) as compared to low molecular weight heparin (LMWH). User can interactively select or deselect SoF for a given clinical outcome by clicking the options in the outcome list (panel on left). The first click selects the clinical outcome and second select deselects the clinical outcome.

Choose measure of effect and denominator for absolute effect.


SUMMARY OF FINDINGS: NETWORK META-ANALYSIS

A Summary of Findings (SoF) table is designed to display multiple comparisons in an interactive manner. All possible combinations in network meta-analysis can be compared using this framework. User can interactively select or deselect SoF for a given clinical outcome by clicking the options in the outcome list (panel on left). The first click selects the clinical outcome and second select deselects the clinical outcome.
